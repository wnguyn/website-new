Summer doesn't behave like other seasons. Winter and its cold, from my experience, have a much simpler quality to it. There's a reason why nostalgia clusters around summer more than any other season: school ends, structure loosens, and the days stop being assigned to you. For many, nobody's most treasured memory during the summer is a big event, but rather a specific moment. Summer teaches you early on in life that much of the good stuff in life mostly arrives unscheduled.

I'm currently writing this inside against the heat of a typical summer. In actuality, most of us in the south spend most of our actual days rotting wherever HVAC may be. As a kid, I often craved summer, but eventually felt relieved in early September when the heat died down.

Although, our memory doesn't grade on how eventful something was. I think it grades on how we felt at that moment, so boredom often feels different in hindsight. The lazy stretch of a summer, the days you'd have called uneventful while you were living them, are usually the ones we miss the most later.

I think no other album encapsulates my experience with summer the way Wildflower does. Most of its strong moments are front-loaded, resembling the joy of the first weeks of June. Through tracks such as "Subways" and "If I Was A Folkstar", The Avalanches establish the initial joy of freedom that entails with summer.

Many tracks from the middle section would be usually something I'd call filler; resembling much of my late summer days where I rotted away in elementary school playing Roblox or whatever. Many of the interludes can at face value, come off as filler. Wildflower's title track essentially is a a "boring" minute interlude, which sharply differs from their previous album "Since I Left You".

However, by the time tracks "Stepkids" and "Saturday Night Inside Out" play, you remember all the highs and lows from summer, and the tracks start to feel less like filler. Hearing the late David Berman, you remember the freedom summer entails such as the millennial humor of "The Noisy Eater" and "Frankie Sinatra". You don't feel the weight of a track while you're listening to it, same as you don't feel the weight of a boring July afternoon.

I think this is what makes Wildflower work as a summer album. From my life experience, Wildflower comes off to me as a summer album rather than an album about summer.
